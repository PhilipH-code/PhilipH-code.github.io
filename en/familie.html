A Munich family

Six houses, one signature

Café Reitschule is part of a family of restaurants that has shaped Munich for decades.

Behind the Reitschule stands a Munich restaurant family with six more houses. Each has its own kitchen and its own neighbourhood. What connects them: the same care.

From the Greek neighbour in our own house to the Japanese robata in the old town. If you know one of the houses, you’ll recognise the others.

Greek, right next door in the Reitschule The Greek neighbour under the same roof: downstairs in the house, with meze, charcoal grill and the Sunday brunch. Reitschule gift cards are valid here too. Schwabing · Website ↗︎ Riviera cuisine on Wittelsbacherplatz The Mediterranean at Wittelsbacher Platz, a few steps from Odeonsplatz. Maxvorstadt · Website ↗︎ Steaks and grill in the Künstlerhaus The family steakhouse in the Künstlerhaus at Lenbachplatz. More than meat: seafood, vegetables and a rooftop terrace with a bar above the square. Maxvorstadt · Website ↗︎ Literaturhaus brasserie on Salvatorplatz The brasserie in the Literaturhaus at Salvatorplatz, named after Oskar Maria Graf. Tall ceilings of a former market hall, a kitchen between Bavarian and French. Altstadt · Website ↗︎ Italian bar and cucina Italy at the Platzl: pizza from the wood-fired oven, pasta and a bar that stretches the evening. Uncomplicated and quite irresistible.
