Privacy Policy

This is a courtesy translation. The German version is legally binding.

1. Privacy at a glance

Protecting your personal data is important to us. We treat your personal data confidentially and in accordance with the statutory data protection regulations (in particular the General Data Protection Regulation, GDPR) and this privacy policy.

This website uses no cookies, no analytics or tracking services and no social media plugins. Fonts and all other content are loaded locally from our server; no content is loaded from third-party servers. For this reason, no cookie banner is required.

The password-protected administration area of this website (staff only, at /admin) uses a technically necessary session cookie for login (Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR). It is set exclusively in this area, contains no personal data and is deleted when the browser is closed. Visitors to the public pages receive no cookies whatsoever.

Reach measurement without cookies

We count page views with our own method operated on our server — without cookies, without third-party tracking services. Only anonymous, aggregated values are recorded: the page visited, the referrer domain, the device type (mobile/desktop) and the time. Your IP address is not stored; to approximately count unique visitors, a short value that changes daily and cannot be reverse-calculated is formed, which can no longer be attributed after 24 hours. The “Do Not Track” signals of your browser are respected. The legal basis is our legitimate interest in reach measurement (Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR); § 25 TDDDG (formerly TTDSG) (German Telecommunications-Telemedia Data Protection Act) is not affected, as nothing is stored on or read from your device.

Meta Pixel (only with consent)

If you consent to the “Marketing” category in the cookie notice, we use the Meta Pixel of Meta Platforms Ireland Ltd. to measure the success of advertisements (Art. 6 (1) (a) GDPR, § 25 (1) TDDDG). In doing so, data is transmitted to Meta, possibly also to the USA (basis: the EU-US Data Privacy Framework, which Meta has joined). Without your consent, the pixel is not loaded. You can withdraw your consent at any time via “Cookie settings” in the footer. Details: Meta privacy policy.

Consent management (cookie settings)

Via the notice on your first visit and at any time via the “Cookie settings” link in the footer, you can decide whether external content (such as fonts from third-party servers) may be loaded (§ 25 (1) TDDDG, Art. 6 (1) (a) GDPR). Without your consent, no connections to third-party servers are established. We store your decision exclusively locally in your browser (localStorage, entry “rs-consent” with a timestamp); it is not tracking and is not transmitted to us or to third parties. Withdrawal is possible at any time with the same ease via the same link (Art. 7 (3) GDPR). Technically necessary storage (this consent itself, the display state of notices for the duration of your visit) takes place on the basis of § 25 (2) no. 2 TDDDG.

3. Data collection on this website

3.1 Server log files

When you access this website, the hosting provider automatically collects information in so-called server log files, which your browser transmits automatically. These are:

  • browser type and browser version
  • operating system used
  • referrer URL
  • host name of the accessing computer
  • time of the server request
  • IP address

This data is not merged with other data sources. It is collected on the basis of Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR. The website operator has a legitimate interest in the technically error-free presentation and the security of its website; for this purpose, the server log files must be recorded. The log files are deleted automatically after a short time.

3.2 Contact by email or telephone

If you contact us by email or telephone (e.g. for reservations, gift cards or event enquiries), your details, including the contact data you provide, are stored by us in order to process the enquiry and in case of follow-up questions. We do not pass on this data without your consent.

Processing takes place on the basis of Art. 6 (1) (b) GDPR, provided your enquiry is related to the performance of a contract or is necessary to carry out pre-contractual measures. In all other cases, processing is based on our legitimate interest in the effective handling of enquiries addressed to us (Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR). The data is deleted as soon as it is no longer required to achieve the purpose for which it was collected and no statutory retention obligations prevent deletion.

3.3 Reservations via OpenTable

For online reservations, we link to the external service OpenTable (OpenTable International Ltd.). Data processing only begins when you follow the link and enter data on the OpenTable website. The OpenTable privacy policy then applies: https://www.opentable.de/legal/privacy-policy. On this website itself, no data is transmitted to OpenTable through the link.

3.4 External links

Our website contains links to external third-party websites (e.g. Instagram, Facebook, Google Maps, partner establishments). When you access our website, no data is transmitted to these providers. Only when you click a link do you leave our offer; from that point on, the privacy provisions of the respective provider apply.

3.5 Newsletter

If you would like to subscribe to our newsletter, we need your email address. Providing your first and last name is voluntary and serves only for a personal salutation. Registration takes place using the so-called double opt-in procedure: after you sign up, we send you an email with a confirmation link. Only when you click this link do we add your address to the mailing list. This ensures that no one can sign up with someone else's address.

To prove the sign-up, we store the time of registration and of confirmation as well as the IP address used. This data serves exclusively to prove consent and to deliver the newsletter. The newsletter informs you about events, changing menus and offers of Café Reitschule.

Processing takes place on the basis of your consent (Art. 6 (1) (a) GDPR). You can withdraw this consent at any time with effect for the future by clicking the unsubscribe link at the end of every newsletter email or by sending us an informal message to contact@cafe-reitschule.de. After you unsubscribe, your address is removed from the active mailing list. We store the sign-up and unsubscribe data on our own server; no transfer to third parties takes place.

3.6 Event inquiries via the form

For inquiries about celebrations and events we offer an inquiry form. We process the details you provide: name and email address, and optionally phone number, preferred date, number of guests, occasion, room preference and your message. We use these details solely to handle and answer your inquiry; you also receive an automatic confirmation of receipt by email.

The legal basis is Art. 6 (1) (b) GDPR (pre-contractual measures). Your inquiry is delivered to our event team by email and is not stored in a database. We delete the correspondence once it is no longer required for handling your request and no statutory retention obligations apply. It is not shared with third parties.

4. Your rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • right of access (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
  • right to restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • right to object to processing (Art. 21 GDPR)

If you have given consent to data processing, you can withdraw it at any time with effect for the future (Art. 7 (3) GDPR). To exercise your rights, an informal message by email to contact@cafe-reitschule.de is sufficient.

Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

In the event of breaches of data protection law, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR). The competent supervisory authority for us is the Bavarian State Office for Data Protection Supervision (BayLDA), Promenade 18, 91522 Ansbach, www.lda.bayern.de.

5. SSL / TLS encryption

For security reasons and to protect the transmission of confidential content, this site uses SSL or TLS encryption. You can recognise an encrypted connection by the fact that the address bar of the browser changes from “http://” to “https://” and by the lock symbol in your browser bar. When encryption is activated, the data you transmit to us cannot be read by third parties.

We point out that data transmission over the internet (e.g. when communicating by unencrypted email) can have security gaps. Complete protection of the data against access by third parties is not possible.

6. Currency of this privacy policy

This privacy policy is currently valid (as of July 2026). As our website develops or due to changed statutory or regulatory requirements, it may become necessary to adapt this privacy policy.
